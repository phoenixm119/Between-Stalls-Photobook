The Windhoek Markets
APRIL 2026  ·  MARSHE-LEE VAN WYK

A photographic exploration of Windhoek's markets - the Bio Market, Village Market, and Oshetu Community Market - through the lens of human exchange. These images capture hands, glances, and gestures, the small transactions that form the quiet architecture of daily life.

By Marshe-lee Van Wyk
01
The Windhoek Bio-Market / Green Market
Quiet Exchange
Before a face is remembered, there is the hand. Open, waiting, reaching across a table for something small enough to hold and large enough to matter. A coin warms in a palm. A paper bag folds around fruit. Fingers brush for a second and then separate, each carrying their own part of the morning. Here, exchange is quiet. Not dramatic, not loud, just the ordinary miracle of one thing leaving and another arriving. The market moves from hand to hand, through touch, through habit, through the soft agreement that this is how a day continues.
02
The Windhoek Village Market
Where Connection Links
At this market, voices arrive before the body does. People lean closer here, not only to look but to listen, to answer, to become part of the rhythm of being among others. A question is asked. A story is slipped in beside a price. Someone laughs, someone lingers, someone leaves carrying more than they came for. Because some things are bought, and some things are simply exchanged in the warmth of attention, in the ease of being noticed, in the brief comfort of a shared moment between strangers who no longer feel like strangers.
03
The Windhoek Oshetu Market
Movement to Profit
Morning does not wait here. It begins already moving. A kind of quiet endurance lives in these motions, in the focus of those who know that survival is often made from repetition. Here, work is not hidden. It stands in the open, under sun, under dust, under the weight of the day, and still continues. By the time the market is full, someone has already earned the right to be tired.

About Artist
Artist Statement
MARSHE-LEE
VAN WYK
I am a Namibian based creative working at the intersection of photography and visual storytelling. My work explores everyday spaces and human experiences, with a focus on emo tion, identity, and connection. Through a combination of documentary imagery and poetic writing, I aim to capture moments that are often overlooked but deeply felt. Windhoek Markets reflects my interest in the subtle exchanges that shape daily life, present ing markets not only as places of trade, but as spaces of community, labour, and shared experience
- MVW, April 2026
